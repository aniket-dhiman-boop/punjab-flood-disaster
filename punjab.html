üìä Flood Data & Analysis

Punjab has been severely affected by recurring floods, impacting thousands of villages, displacing residents, and destroying farmland. Understanding the data allows authorities and citizens to plan effective mitigation strategies. Here‚Äôs a snapshot of the flood impact and patterns:

Water Levels & Rivers

Major rivers like Sutlej and Beas often overflow due to heavy monsoon rains. Water levels have risen over 3 meters in certain districts, inundating nearby villages and towns.

Rainfall Statistics

During peak monsoon, rainfall in Punjab exceeded 250mm within 24 hours in multiple locations. This led to flash floods and significant drainage issues in urban and rural areas.

Economic Loss

Damage to crops, houses, and infrastructure is estimated in billions of INR. Agriculture is the most affected sector, with paddy and wheat crops submerged for weeks.

Displacement & Relief

Over 384,000 people were temporarily displaced. Relief operations include providing shelter, clean water, food, and medical aid to affected populations.

Analyzing these patterns helps implement early warning systems, effective drainage improvements, and timely evacuation plans to minimize loss of life and property.

Floods pose serious risks to life and property. Following preventive measures can help you and your family stay safe during flood events:

Before a Flood

Keep emergency kits ready with water, food, medicines, flashlights, and important documents. Elevate valuables and electronics to higher floors. Monitor weather updates regularly.

During a Flood

Avoid walking or driving through floodwater. Seek higher ground immediately. Stay informed through radio, TV, or official apps. Do not touch electrical equipment if wet.

Evacuation

Follow evacuation orders promptly. Know the nearest shelter locations. Assist children, elderly, and neighbors if necessary.

After a Flood

Return home only when authorities declare it safe. Avoid floodwater as it may be contaminated. Inspect your home for damages before entering and maintain hygiene to prevent diseases.

Awareness and preparedness are key to reducing the impact of floods. Stay safe, stay informed, and support your community in times of need.
